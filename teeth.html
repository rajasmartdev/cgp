Why Choose Teeth Braces?

Teeth braces are not just about aesthetics they improve:

  • Teeth alignment
  • Bite correction
  • Jaw positioning
  • Oral hygiene
  • Speech clarity
  • Long-term dental health

Properly aligned teeth reduce the risk of cavities, gum disease, and jaw pain. Braces are an investment in both your smile and your overall oral health.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to your most common braces questions

At what age can I get braces?

There's no age limit for braces! Children, teenagers, and adults can all benefit from orthodontic treatment. In children, early treatment can guide jaw growth, while in adults, braces can achieve excellent results regardless of age.

How long does treatment typically take?

Treatment duration varies based on the complexity of your case. Most treatments range from 6 months to 3 years. Mild cases may be completed faster, while complex cases requiring jaw correction take longer. We'll provide a precise timeline during your consultation.

Do braces hurt?

You may experience slight discomfort during the first few days after braces are applied or adjusted, similar to a gentle pressure on your teeth. This is completely normal and temporary. Most patients adapt quickly, and modern braces are more comfortable than ever.

Can I eat normally with braces?

With traditional braces, you should avoid hard, sticky, or chewy foods that could damage them. Removable aligners can be taken out during meals, allowing you to eat normally. We provide a detailed list of foods to avoid and what you can safely enjoy.

Are invisible braces as effective as metal braces?

Modern invisible options like Invisalign are highly effective for mild to moderate cases. Traditional metal or ceramic braces are more effective for severe cases requiring precise three-dimensional control. We'll recommend the best option based on your specific needs.

What is the retention process after braces are removed?

After braces removal, you'll wear a custom retainer to maintain your beautiful new smile. We typically recommend wearing retainers full-time initially, then transitioning to nights only. This prevents your teeth from shifting back to their original positions.
